Lisbon

🌱 Seedling Visited May 2026 Tended 5 Jul 2026

Lisbon is a city built on seven hills and, apparently, on the assumption that you'd rather not carry change.

I came for the tiles and the light and left thinking about payments, because of course I did. The thing that stuck with me wasn't a monument — it was Tram 28, a wooden antique that grinds up through Alfama packed with tourists and locals, where I watched an elderly woman tap a contactless card against a reader older than most of my colleagues. A hundred-year-old vehicle, a two-second payment. That contrast is the whole trip in miniature.

Down at the till of a tiny pastelaria, the pattern held: card first, cash grudgingly. The card machine came out before I'd finished asking. It's a useful reminder that "cashless" isn't one global switch — it's a thousand small merchant decisions about which is less hassle, and Lisbon had quietly made its choice.

Observational note, filed for later: the places that go cashless fastest aren't always the richest — they're the ones where card acceptance got cheap and easy enough that the friction flipped. I keep meaning to write that up properly.
