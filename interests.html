Interests

Things I geek out on

Low-pressure and human — the stuff that fills my off-hours and my open browser tabs. Some of these will grow into their own pages; most are happy just being a line here.

  • Payment-system archaeology

    Reading old network rulebooks and settlement specs the way other people read history books. The "why is it like this?" is almost always more interesting than the "what".

  • Trams and old public transit

    Give me a century-old tram with a modern fare reader and I'm happy for an afternoon. See Lisbon.

  • Maps & cartography

    How a place chooses to draw itself — what it centers, what it leaves off — tells you what it values. Same instinct as reading a system diagram.

  • Hand-drawn diagrams & explanation

    I think with a pen. The little wobbly flow diagrams around this site are the honest artifact of how I actually understand things.

  • Commonplace-booking

    Collecting definitions, quotes, and half-thoughts and letting them cross-pollinate. This whole site is basically that habit, made public — see the Notes.

  • Sourdough

    A slow feedback loop with living inputs and non-deterministic output. Suspiciously similar to running a system in production.

  • Strategy board games

    Anything with hidden information and a good economy. I like games that are really arguments about incentives wearing a fun hat.

Want to swap notes on any of these? The door's open.
