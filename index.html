How money moves — and who moves it.

A working notebook at the intersection of three things: the plumbing of payment systems, the geopolitics of the rails that carry money, and AI as leverage over financial systems.

This isn't a résumé. It's a commonplace book — part long-form essay, part short atomic note — that I tend over time. Some pieces are finished; many are seedlings; a few are just stubs. I think in public here, so expect ideas to grow, get pruned, and cross-link as I learn.

  • Payments under the hood. What actually happens between tapping a card and money landing in a merchant's account.
  • The geopolitics of money. Who owns the rails, who can switch them off, and why that is quietly one of the most important forms of power.
  • AI as leverage. Where machine intelligence genuinely changes the economics of moving, checking, and governing money — and where it's just hype.
