About

Colophon Last tended 5 Jul 2026

I work on the systems that move money. Day to day that means leading engineering teams; in my head it means being pleasantly stuck on three questions at once. This site is where I think them through in the open.

The first question is how payments actually work beneath the surface. Tapping a card feels instant, but underneath it's a small relay race — authorize, clear, settle — run by a chain of players who never meet, on clocks that don't agree. I find the gaps between those steps endlessly interesting, because that's where the risk, the money, and the good engineering problems hide.

The second is who controls the world's payment rails, and why. The networks that carry money are quiet infrastructure until the moment someone is removed from one — and then you realize access to the rails is a form of power, and that a surprising amount of geopolitics runs through plumbing most people never see.

The third is how AI changes the economics of running financial systems. Not the sci-fi version — the practical one. Where does machine intelligence actually lower the cost of moving, checking, and reconciling money, and where is "AI" just doing rhetorical work? I'm more interested in the boring, load-bearing answers than the flashy ones.

How I think

If there's a through-line to my work, it's a stubborn belief that complex things can be made legible without being dumbed down. I lead teams best when I can draw the system on a whiteboard and have everyone — engineer or not — leave the room with the same mental model. Teaching in public is just that instinct pointed outward: if I can explain how money moves clearly enough for a newcomer, I usually understand it better myself. That's the whole point of this garden.

I try to lead with the point, define the jargon the first time it shows up, cut the fluff, and reach for a diagram whenever words start to strain. You'll see me revise things here in the open — changing my mind in public is a feature, not a bug.

A quick note on what you won't find

No job titles, employer names, or war stories from specific projects. I keep everything here conceptual and general, illustrated with public examples. The ideas travel further than any résumé would.

How this garden works

A few deliberate mechanics, in the digital-garden spirit:

  • Everything is meant to be edited. Pages carry a “last tended” date and a growth label — Stub 🌱 Seedling Evergreen — so stubs are a feature, not an apology. I optimize for adding a card or essay in minutes, not for a rigid publishing pipeline.
  • POSSE. "Publish on your Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere." Essays are canonical here — this is the source of record — and I syndicate them to Substack with a link back.
  • A curated feed. The RSS feed carries only things I think are worth your attention — new essays and notable notes — not every typo-fix or trivial edit.
  • Traverse by tag or search. The tags page links everything by interest and has a quick search box, so you can follow a thread across essays and notes.
  • A visible changelog records the edits worth announcing.

If any of this resonates, the best place to start is the Writing — and if it sparks a thought, a disagreement, or a better example, please reach out. Other people's ideas are how a garden grows.
