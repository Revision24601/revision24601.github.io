How money moves

🌱 Seedling Created 28 Jun 2026 Last tended 5 Jul 2026

Most people say "I paid" as if it were one instant event. Under the hood it's at least three: authorization, clearing, and settlement. They run on different clocks, and almost everything interesting about payments — fraud, disputes, float, fees — lives in the gaps between them.

Quick definitions, since the jargon does real work here:

  • Authorization — a fast "yes/no": does this account have the funds and does it look legitimate? Money doesn't move yet; it's just earmarked.
  • Clearing — the parties reconcile who owes whom, netting many transactions against each other.
  • Settlement — the actual transfer of value between banks, so the merchant is genuinely paid.
The float — that quiet window between "authorized" and "settled" — is where a surprising amount of the payments business, and its risk, actually sits.
